The Union:
The Union unites all workers who earn their living through work, without exploiting the labor of others, in an ongoing and relentless struggle to protect their employment, improve their status, wages, social benefits, and working conditions, and to promote the quality of life, the quality of life, and the equality of opportunities for workers and their families. It fosters mutual cooperation and assistance, ensures that work and workers are valued, and builds a democratic, free, progressive, and humane society based on the principles of social justice, striving for equality and the protection of individual freedoms in matters of religion and worldview, without discrimination based on race or gender, and seeks peace.

The Union's functions and scope of activity:
A. Addressing professional, settlement, and economic issues, and promoting cultural and educational activities for workers.
B. Ensuring organized employment in all workplaces, establishing professional unions for all workers in their respective fields, and establishing national federations in various industries.
C. Encouraging immigration and social and cultural integration.
D. Promoting the national economy and increasing productivity.
E. Developing the workers' economy in agriculture (workers' settlements, groups, and kibbutzim), industry, crafts, construction, transportation, and other industries in rural and urban areas.
F. Promoting and developing cooperatives of all types in all sectors of the economy.
G. Organizing the supply and consumption of goods for workers and marketing their products on cooperative principles.
H. Establishing institutions and foundations to finance the workers' economy in agriculture, industry, and other sectors.
I. Protecting consumers through consumer protection activities.
J. Establishing institutions and mutual funds: health funds, insurance and pension funds, and other funds designed to promote mutual assistance among workers.
K. Promoting cultural and social activities among workers: instilling the values of the labor movement, promoting the Hebrew language, publishing literature and newspapers, establishing cultural and educational institutions, and meeting the needs of religious members, general education and professional training institutions, and promoting physical activity among workers through the "Hapoel" physical education association.
L. Organizing the movement of women workers (the Union) as an integral part.
M. The Union:
Organizing the Union, fostering the pioneering spirit among youth, and providing assistance to pioneering youth movements in the country, which educate their members in work, settlement, and membership in the workers' organization, and maintaining ties with the diaspora.
N. Organizing and encouraging the pioneering movement, encouraging pioneering youth movements abroad, and maintaining ties with them.
O. Maintaining relationships with Jewish workers' organizations and movements in the diaspora, with international labor movements, and participating in international professional workers' organizations.
To fulfill its functions and activities, the Union may undertake any action as detailed below:
A. Managing all real estate and movable property, renting them, selling them, and granting rights and licenses in them. Establishing, developing, improving, managing, leasing, building, buying, renting, holding, acquiring, and obtaining any rights and licenses in any way, and acquiring any rights in real estate and movable property. Founding or participating in the founding, establishing, and merging any corporation or legal entity. Acquiring by signature, purchase, exchange, or any other means shares, bonds, debentures, obligations, guarantees, and securities issued or issued by any corporation or legal entity, both in Israel and in any other country, holding, managing, pledging, mortgaging, selling, transferring, and engaging in any manner in shares, bonds, bond portfolios, debentures, guarantees, and securities, guaranteeing and insuring capital, dividends, and interest on them. Performing all duties of a trustee and performing all duties and actions related thereto. Establishing, managing, founding any enterprise, company, corporation, and real estate, financing, acting as a licensee, contractor, investor, agent, undertaking and performing any action or transaction that may assist, directly or indirectly, in achieving these goals, all or some of them. Lending, obtaining, and guaranteeing the payment of any sum of money in any manner and under any conditions deemed appropriate, and also guaranteeing the repayment of any loans by providing mortgages, pledges, and collateral on all its property and assets, and redeeming and extinguishing any such mortgages, pledges, or collateral.
Y. Entering into any contract or agreement and signing any document, deed, contract, and agreement within the scope of its functions and activities.
Z. Insuring all property and activities and/or positions held therein, in whole or in part, or indemnifying against any loss, damage, risk, or liability that is insurable or indemnifiable under any law, whether by providing an advance indemnity or by providing an indemnity retroactively.
AA. Investing and managing its liquid funds in a timely manner for business purposes in the manner determined by the board from time to time.
BB. Performing all actions related to and associated with its functions and scope of activity, or that may assist, facilitate, or contribute in any way to fulfilling its functions, in whole or in part.
CC. Performing any legal or other action that may be beneficial to it.
The General Cooperative Association of Workers in the Land of Israel (hereinafter referred to as "the Workers' Company") is a cooperative association comprising all members of the Union. The Workers' Company is responsible for carrying out the economic and financial activities of the Union and of all its members.

The Workers' Association will operate to achieve social goals in the areas of education, culture, sports, health, worker protection, welfare, and other union objectives. To achieve these goals, the Workers' Association may establish a reserve fund from its surplus assets and profits, and make the accumulated funds available to the union. The chairperson of the Workers' Association will also serve as the chairperson of the union, and it will be managed by the union's governing bodies, or by those appointed by them. An internal auditor will audit the Workers' Association and the union. Within the union, there will be a Workers' Association. Any citizen or resident or foreign worker employed legally in Israel, or a resident of the Palestinian Authority employed legally in Israel, who is at least eighteen years old, and who agrees to accept the Workers' Association, its principles, and the decisions of its authorized bodies, will be a member of the Workers' Association. Membership in the union is voluntary and personal and is not transferable. Each member of the union belongs to a regional branch based on their place of residence within the union's jurisdiction, and is entitled to enjoy its services. Each member of the union is entitled to join a professional union relevant to their profession and to enjoy its services. Each member of the union is entitled to receive professional union services from the regional branch where their place of work is located. Each member of the union is required to pay membership dues as determined from time to time by the union's governing bodies. Each member of the union has the right to vote for and be elected to the various union bodies, subject to the provisions of this charter and its regulations, including provisions regarding eligibility for election, which are intended to maintain ethical standards and prevent bias. a. Each member of the union has the right to receive information about the union's activities, the decisions of its bodies, its budgets, and published union reports, which relate to these matters. b. Each member of the union has the right to enjoy the services of the union and its bodies, subject to the terms and conditions established from time to time. It should be noted that membership in the union does not confer any right to profit or other monetary compensation beyond its services. Any citizen or resident or foreign worker employed legally in Israel, or a resident of the Palestinian Authority as mentioned in section 1, who wishes to become a member of the union, must submit an application form, and their membership will become effective upon receipt of the application form. It should be noted that the application form may be submitted manually to representatives of the professional union or the employer relations and personnel department (hereinafter referred to as "the manual application form"), it may be submitted electronically (hereinafter referred to as "the online application form"), it may be completed by phone with a union representative (hereinafter referred to as "the telephone application form"), and in any other way permitted by the union in its discretion. Membership will become effective upon receipt of the form. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Membership Committee, as defined below, may order that membership not be granted for a period of six months from the date of receipt of the application form or from the date of receipt of the application form by the internet server, provided that a manual, online, or telephone application form submitted to the union before the effective date of this amendment and that complies with the provisions of this section shall be deemed to have been completed in accordance with the provisions of this section. 2.2. The member has the right to appeal the decision of the Membership Committee to the union, and the decision on the appeal will be final. The appeal must be filed within 30 days of receipt of the decision of the Membership Committee. The union will have a Membership Committee consisting of: the chairperson of the union or their representative, who will serve as the chairperson of the committee; a representative of the union; and the CEO of the union or their representative. A member of the union shall not be a member of any other workers' organization in a situation of conflict of interest. Membership in the union will terminate upon the occurrence of any of the following events: a. The death of the member. b. The member's notification of their intention to terminate their membership in the union. The notification will become effective immediately upon submission to representatives of the professional union, the employer relations and personnel department, or the union to which the worker belongs, or in any other manner as determined by the union's procedures. c. A decision of the Membership Committee, made after hearing the member's arguments, for one of the following reasons: 1. It is determined that the application for membership, as mentioned in section 8, was made without consent or contains false information; 2. The member acted maliciously against the union or acted in violation of the provisions of this charter; 3. The member is in arrears in paying membership dues as required by section 6 above and the decisions of the union's bodies based on it, and has not settled the debt after receiving a notice regarding it. 4. A member who believes that they have been harmed by the decision of the Membership Committee as mentioned in subparagraph (c) may appeal the decision to the union within 30 days of receiving the decision of the Membership Committee. The decision of the union on the appeal will be final. Any citizen or resident or foreign worker who submitted an application for membership in the union before the effective date of this amendment, including through the union's website, will be considered a member of the union for all purposes, even if approval for membership has not been received by the Membership Committee as provided in section 8 of chapter 3 of the union. Union - Part 1: Chairman of the Union
1. The union shall be chaired by a chairman elected by the union.
2. The chairman of the union is authorized to:
a. Conduct meetings of the union to make general decisions, address issues related to labor relations, working conditions, and employee rights, and to conclude agreements within its jurisdiction, including collective bargaining agreements and agreements to resolve disputes, including arbitration agreements.
b. Declare and handle labor disputes and strikes in all sectors of the economy.
c. Order legal action to be taken on behalf of the union.
Within this authority, the chairman of the union is authorized to resolve disputes between employee committees, management, and/or professional unions regarding the initiation of legal proceedings in the labor court. The chairman of the union may, at the request of an employee committee, order the filing of a request for participation in a collective dispute before the union, even if the request contradicts the position of the professional union or the regional body. The chairman of the union may also order that the proceedings in the labor court be conducted by the employee committee or by the union.
3. If the chairman of the union decides not to grant a request from an employee committee as described above, the employee committee may file a request with the union to participate in a collective dispute regarding the implementation and interpretation of the agreement, provided that it complies with the following:
a. The request must state that it is submitted by an employee committee on behalf of the union.
b. The request must specify that the preliminary conditions for its submission by the employee committee have been met, as required by this constitution.
c. The union will be added as a respondent to this request.
d. To serve as an arbitrator in disputes arising between the bodies of the union.
e. To advocate for the interests of employees.
f. To recommend to the union the establishment of professional unions, mergers or consolidations between unions, and the regulation of their powers.

E. "Union" means a professional or sectoral union.

1. Employees, including retirees, may form professional, sectoral, or general unions, as provided in this section.

2. A. 4. A. Membership in a sectoral union does not preclude membership in a professional union, and vice versa, but no employee may be a member of more than two unions: one professional union and one sectoral union.

A professional union is authorized to declare a labor dispute and/or approve a strike affecting the employees in the profession or sector it represents. If the chair of the union objects in writing to the declaration of the labor dispute and/or the strike, the implementation will be suspended for a period not exceeding 45 days (including the advance notice period required by the law on settling labor disputes) for review between the union, the professional union, and the employers. The professional union may request the chair of the union to shorten the suspension period and the review. If the professional union receives a qualified notice from the union stating that it has withdrawn its objection to the strike within the suspension period, the declaration of the labor dispute and/or the strike as described in section 8 above will take effect immediately, for all purposes and in accordance with the law. If a resolution of the differences between the professional union and the union cannot be reached, the matter will be referred to the mediation and arbitration committee no later than the end of the suspension and review period. These provisions do not apply to a professional union that has an autonomy agreement with the union in its current or former name.

5. Funding of Factions
a. Factions within the union are entitled to funding for their ongoing expenses, as defined in Section 18 below, provided that the union operates in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 2 and the guidelines for the financial management of the factions, which are an integral part of the union. Each faction is entitled to funding for its ongoing expenses each month, starting from the date the election results are announced and continuing until the date the results of the subsequent election are announced. If a constituent group has a dispute that includes provisions regarding the distribution of funding for ongoing expenses, each constituent group will be considered a separate faction for all purposes, provided that the constituent group has submitted the agreement between it and the parent faction to the union.
c. The funding for a faction's ongoing expenses will be calculated proportionally based on the election results.
d. The amount of funding per unit and any changes to it will be determined annually as part of the union's budget, as approved by the authorized bodies.
e. Ongoing expenses of a faction include expenses incurred in connection with organizing its activities, propaganda, and public relations, as well as organizational and ideological activities as a union faction, including liabilities arising from such expenses. These expenses are considered ongoing expenses, provided that they are incurred solely for union activities that are consistent with the goals and functions of the union, as defined in its charter.
(a1) A faction may not pay wages to any person, directly or indirectly, including through contractual agreements, at a rate exceeding the average wage in the economy, except for a period of 6 months prior to the elections for the union's elected bodies. Furthermore, a faction may not employ or contract with union members, their relatives, or a company controlled by them, or enter into contracts with relatives of the faction's employees or a company controlled by them.
(2) For the purposes of this section, "relatives" include spouses, siblings, parents, grandparents, children, and also children, siblings, or parents of a spouse or a relative of a member of the union.
b. Expenses incurred by a faction for illegal activities, activities that could harm the union, or expenses incurred in violation of Section (a1) above will not be considered ongoing expenses, and the faction will be required to reimburse the union for any funds it received and used for those expenses. The reimbursement of funds may be made through a deduction from the faction's funding.
c. The chairperson of the faction and the authorized signatories of the faction must sign a declaration stating that the funds will be used in accordance with the provisions of this section. The form of the declaration will be determined by the union and approved by the union.
9. Prohibition on Assignment
a. A faction is not permitted to assign its right to receive ongoing funding, in whole or in part, and any such assignment will not be valid.
b. Notwithstanding the foregoing, a faction may instruct that ongoing funding be transferred to a bank, another union faction, or a political party, provided that this is intended to repay a loan received from that bank, union faction, or political party for expenses incurred for its elections or ongoing activities.
10. Restriction on Income
A faction may not receive, directly or indirectly, any contribution from any individual or entity, whether domestic or foreign. For the purposes of this provision, "entity" includes a registered partnership and a legal entity.
11. Funding of Preliminary Elections
Notwithstanding Section 20 above, a faction that holds preliminary elections to determine its candidates for the union's elected bodies within a body with more than 500 voters may collect fees from the candidates in those preliminary elections to fund the preliminary election process, provided that the total amount collected does not exceed the cost of conducting the preliminary elections.
12. Submission of Annual Report
a. Once a year, the faction, through its chairperson, must submit to the union, as well as to the union's legal counsel, an annual report on the use of funds received from the union. The report must be prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles and must be approved by a professional.
b. The annual report must be submitted by the last day of March or, at the latest, by the last day of June of the fiscal year following the fiscal year to which the annual report relates.
13. Findings of the Report
a. Within four months of the date specified in Section (21b) above, the finance department will submit to the chairperson of the union, the chairperson of the union, and the chairperson of the faction a report on the review of the factions' annual reports, which will indicate:
1. Which factions did not submit the annual report on time or at all.
2. Which factions managed their accounts in accordance with this charter and the guidelines for the financial management of factions.
3. Which factions did not manage their accounts in accordance with this charter and the guidelines for the financial management of factions, and the deficiencies that were identified.
b. If the union determines that a faction has not submitted the annual report, the chairperson of the union will send a written notice to the chairperson of the faction, instructing them to submit the annual report within seven days. If the faction does not comply, ongoing funding will be suspended immediately, and the provisions of Section 25 will apply.
c. If material deficiencies are found in the reports submitted by the factions, the chairperson of the union will, at his discretion, submit the report to the internal auditor of the union, who will submit his findings to the chairperson of the union within four months of the date he received the report.
d. Upon receipt of the auditor's report, the chairperson of the union will, at his discretion, submit the report to the union's legal counsel, who will submit his recommendations to the chairperson of the union within 30 days.
e. Upon receipt of the legal counsel's recommendations, the chairperson of the union will present the auditor's report and the legal counsel's recommendations to the union, and the union is authorized to make one or more of the following decisions:
1. Grant an extension for submitting a revised report based on the recommendations of the auditor and the legal counsel.

2.  Suspension of funding until the identified deficiencies are rectified.
3.  Instruction regarding the disbursement of funds for expenses that were not recognized as current expenses, as mentioned in section 18 above, including an instruction to the financial department to deduct from the regular funding the amounts that the faction is obligated to repay.
4.  Authority of the Association.
    a.  The authority of the auditor to conduct audits of factions, as stipulated in the constitution, shall not be affected by the authority of any other authorized body to conduct audits of factions or bodies affiliated with them, whether or not such an audit has been conducted by that body, and regardless of the results of such an audit.
    b.  Nothing in this section shall diminish any right, law, regulation, or procedure, including the authority of the Association to conduct audits of a faction at any time it deems appropriate, even if the faction has submitted its reports as required.
5.  Faction that has ceased to exist.
    A faction that has ceased to exist is a faction that is not represented in the Association or in the Federation. If a faction ceases to exist for any reason and has funds or assets acquired with funds received from the Association, it must use these funds or assets in accordance with the instructions detailed below, and the following guidelines apply to the financial management of factions:
    1.  Its affairs shall continue to be managed in accordance with its regulations, as amended.
    2.  The use of funds or assets shall be solely for the purpose of organizing activities, propaganda, and public relations, and for maintaining the organizational and ideological connection with the public, provided that the use of funds or assets is solely for activities that are consistent with the goals and functions of the Association, as defined in its constitution.
    3.  It must submit an annual report to the internal auditor of the Association regarding the use of funds or assets received from the Association and remaining in its possession. A copy of the report must be submitted to the Association. The report must be prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles and approved by a professional. The annual report must be submitted by the last day of March and no later than the last day of June of the fiscal year following the fiscal year to which the annual report relates.
    4.  The Association has the authority to conduct an audit, as determined by the Association, regardless of whether another authorized body has the authority to conduct an audit.
5.  The faction must provide the Association with a list containing its full registered address, the names and signatures of the authorized signatories on its bank account, the details of the bank accounts where funds are held, and a list of assets acquired with funds from the Association and remaining in its possession. Any change or update to the list must be immediately notified to the financial department of the Association.
6.  The funds must be managed solely in bank accounts in Israel.
7.  Penalties.
    a.  A faction that ceases to exist and does not comply with the provisions of this section or the guidelines for the financial management of factions may be subject to a complaint filed by the Chairman of the Association with the legal advisor of the Association, who will notify the parties involved of the complaint and schedule a hearing.
    b.  If the Association, after the hearing, finds that there is a basis for filing a lawsuit against the faction, its chairman, or any official responsible for the violation, the Association will file a lawsuit, which will be heard by a panel of three judges, and the Association will be authorized to impose one or more of the following penalties:
    1.  Suspension of funding for current expenses from the faction for a limited or indefinite period.
    2.  Repayment of funds that were disbursed in violation of the constitution or the guidelines for the financial management of factions.
    3.  One or more of the penalties or disciplinary measures specified in section 8 of Chapter XIII of the constitution.
Guidelines for the financial management of factions.
In these guidelines, "faction" means a faction as defined in section 1 of the chapter on factions in the Association.
General guideline:
1.  A faction must maintain an accounting system that allows, in accordance with generally accepted auditing standards, for the clear identification, verification, and reconciliation of its current expenses, and for the identification of its revenues by source.
2.  The records of income and expenses of a faction must be maintained separately and independently of any other entity, including its parent faction.
3.  The accounting records must be maintained on a current and up-to-date basis and must include all financial transactions.
4.  Each expense and income must be recorded in its entirety, including expenses that have been paid and income that has been received, but for which there is an obligation, and all must be supported by proper documentation.
5.  Each entry in the accounting system must refer to a supporting document that verifies the reliability and completeness of the entry and allows for the identification of the transaction; the entry must allow for the identification of the supporting document in the faction's files.
6.  a. A faction must maintain a separate bank account to which funds received from the Association will be transferred.
    b. The account holder must be the legal entity under which the faction operates, and if the faction is not incorporated as a legal entity, it must be registered with the income tax authorities as a non-legal entity.
    c. The authorized signatories on the bank account of the faction must be those who have been authorized by the faction's management from among its members or officials.
7.  The faction must notify the Association, through its chairman, of three representatives who are authorized to handle all of the faction's financial affairs. The representatives must be members of the faction.
8.  a. Subject to the provisions of subparagraph (b) below, a faction must pay its expenses only by check, drawn on the bank account maintained in its name.
    b. A faction may pay an expense in cash if the total amount of the expense does not exceed 600 New Israeli Shekels per month; the faction must maintain a separate record of such payments, indicating the recipients of the payments and the details of the expense.
9.  Each expense of the faction must be approved by the signatures of at least two authorized signatories, along with the faction's stamp.
10. a. The accounting books and supporting documents of a faction for a given year must be kept by the faction for seven years from the date of submission of the annual report relating to that year, to the auditor.
    b. A faction must immediately provide the Association with any documents that it believes are necessary for the Association to conduct an audit of the faction, within the scope of the Association's authority.
    c.

This section does not diminish the obligation to maintain records in accordance with all other applicable laws, regulations, or guidelines. Documents and Approvals: 1.
