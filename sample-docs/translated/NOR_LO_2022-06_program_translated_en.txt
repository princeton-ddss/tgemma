Introduction The trade union's goal is to increase unionization rates in Norway and to become the largest worker organization in all sectors and occupational groups. The trade union's strength and influence lie in the fact that we are many and that we have a broad organization, and a vibrant member democracy with active members and employee representatives who are given the opportunity to influence and develop the trade union's policies. The trade union's presence through local organizations and the regional forums is very important in order to achieve the trade union's policies. This means that the unions must organize more members, and more unions must join the trade union family. This must be given particular priority in industries with low unionization rates. In order to maintain and strengthen the trade union's position in Norwegian working and social life, the organization must adapt to the changes in working life. The trade union and the unions must work on member retention to demonstrate their relevance to different types of workers. The proportion of employed people with higher education is increasing, and the trade union faces strong competition from other main organizations. The goal is to strengthen the trade union's position and influence within this group. The trade union aims to be the preferred main organization for those with higher education. The trade union should be a main organization that takes overall responsibility for social development, which organizes and mobilizes in all parts of Norwegian working life. The organized working life faces major challenges in the years ahead. The trade union and the unions must have forward-looking strategies to meet these challenges. This means being an organization that understands social changes. Membership must be experienced as relevant and meaningful. The trade union's ORGANIZATIONAL PROGRAM | 5 2. The trade union's own organization The trade union's agenda is primarily shaped by the issues that affect members, their workplaces, and their lives. Changes and adjustments must be made regularly as a result of social development, internal needs, and priorities. The trade union is shaped and shaped by social development, a development that entails major changes in working life. The trade union must be an open, adaptable, and dynamic organization. All activities and projects carried out in the trade union must have clear objectives in order to be able to demonstrate the results of these. The experiences must be used for further development of activities and learning across union boundaries. 2.1 Local and regional work Through a good regional and local organizational structure and a strong administrative presence through our regional offices, the trade union will safeguard and further develop our important role throughout the country. The trade union is an important supporter of labor struggles and strikes in their areas. The trade union will carry out the local organization's year during the period. The regional councils and regional conferences are our organizational tools for informing, involving, coordinating, and creating joint activities in the regions. Local organizations are important geographical associations of trade unions, and work to ensure that they coordinate their professional and political activities. Local organizations are the volunteer work in the trade union that creates local activity and coordinates efforts for a common purpose. The unions in the trade union's decision-making bodies must ensure a holistic picture, a coordinated policy, and organizational measures. It is important that the decisions are well known and reach all parts of the organization. It is very important that the flow of information is improved and strengthened to all levels, and that the knowledge of the trade union and the unions' structure is better known. The core of the trade union's administrative and executive structures is the trade union. These have a broad and diverse range of activities and contribute to safeguarding the trade union's positions in a number of areas.

This applies to areas where the union exercises its social responsibility, such as in economic and employment policy, education and skills policy, and the union's political work, through organization and recruitment, and in the union's coordinating role in the regions. All regions have committees that work to promote the union's work for young members, families, and equality, and the union will work to further develop and strengthen local organizations and regional work to ensure engagement close to where policies are formed. The union will contribute regionally to training on organization, policy, and other relevant topics in events across union boundaries, and will facilitate the union's increased visibility as a social actor in regional work. The union will facilitate the development of systems that make information flow and work in local organizations easier.

**UNION ORGANIZATIONAL PROGRAM | 62.2 Employee Representatives**

The labor movement and workers' rights are the foundation for a well-functioning workplace and a solidary development of society and the labor market. The core task of employee representatives is to ensure that members receive a fair share of the value created, in the form of increased purchasing power, equitable distribution, and social improvements. Employee representatives play an important role in promoting an organized, decent, and productive workplace where the interests of workers and democracy are strengthened. Well-trained, confident employee representatives and active clubs in each workplace are the union's most important member benefit. They are the ones who bring collective bargaining agreements to life, who protect the interests of individual members vis-à-vis employers, and who create activity and engagement in each workplace.

In order for employee representatives to be able to fulfill their roles, it is important that they are both confident and competent in their practice. Employee representatives are increasingly finding that negotiations and dialogue with employers take place with the support of legal expertise. This places stricter demands on employee representatives, as traditional negotiation skills are not always considered sufficient. Many employee representatives report feeling alone in their roles, without a club or network where they can receive support or advice when they need it. It is important for the union that employee representatives are well taken care of, as they are our greatest resource. By using digital tools, the union and its affiliates can strengthen their information work and training for employee representatives, and provide easier access to knowledge and information.

It is a central task for the union and its affiliates to enable employee representatives to perform these tasks in the best possible way through information, training, exchange of experiences across union boundaries, and practical support in their daily work. The union will strengthen its role in the development and implementation of training both nationally and regionally, and in relation to individual projects. The union will contribute to developing platforms for sharing experiences and digital meeting places for employee representatives across industries and sectors. The union will contribute to strengthening the competence of employee representatives and developing digital tools to provide easier access to knowledge and information.

**2.3 A Diverse Membership**

The union wants a society where everyone has a place and opportunities. We believe that it is positive that Norway is a society with great diversity. Although, as a society, we have made great strides in recent decades towards increased acceptance of various minorities, it is still important work to ensure real equality between people regardless of gender, ethnicity, religious and cultural background, disability, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression. The Sami population and various national minorities face challenges related to discrimination that must be taken seriously. Within the union community, people should feel at home and equal. The labor movement should be a safe place to get involved and take responsibility. The goal of equality requires a conscious attitude towards gender balance in boards, committees, and events, which leaders and employee representatives are responsible for following up at all levels. There should be zero tolerance for sexual harassment, and the work on prevention and awareness should be followed up with good systems and procedures. The union will further develop its work to promote positive attitudes against discrimination and racism. We will work to increase knowledge about the challenges faced by minority populations in the workplace, and to create greater understanding of the barriers that people with minority backgrounds still face in the workplace and in the labor movement. An important part of this is to strengthen the organization and training of people with different minority backgrounds. Employee representatives should have better skills in identifying and addressing discrimination on behalf of members.

The union will actively contribute to ensuring that unions recruit and train members and representatives from minority backgrounds, so that they can take on leadership positions in local unions. The union will work to increase knowledge about the challenges faced by minorities in the workplace. The union will work to ensure that the organization is best equipped to prevent sexual harassment.

2.4. The union will support the union's organizational goal of being the leading workers' organization in Norway by contributing to increased membership and greater member loyalty. In addition to collective bargaining agreements, representatives, and active local branches, the union benefits program is an important part of the union's member benefits. The union benefits program will be continued and further developed during the congress period. During this period, the union will explore the possibility of collective pension savings. The goal is for the union benefits program to become the most well-known and preferred benefits program among union members. Members should perceive the benefits as good, attractive, and relevant. The union benefits program will provide members with security in their personal lives and promote a sense of community within the labor movement through communication. The union will further develop the technological platform to reach members and will constantly strive to be as relevant as possible to each individual. The union and the unions own and are responsible for the union benefits program. Members can propose changes to the program or new products through the unions or local branches. The union benefits program will continue to be self-supporting and self-financing. To achieve these goals, the union benefits program must have active and reliable suppliers. They must provide competitive products of good quality, which take into account environmental and sustainability considerations, as well as the union's ethical guidelines. The union benefits program suppliers should, where possible, be companies with collective bargaining agreements. The union will contribute to organizing and increasing member loyalty within the union organizations. The goal is to increase members' and potential members' knowledge of the content of the union benefits program. The goal is to develop new products and services through the union benefits program that assist the unions and representatives in their work to increase the level of unionization.

The union's organizational program.
Membership growth.
3.1. Organization. The union will support the unions' recruitment strategies and work together to further develop cross-disciplinary recruitment efforts. Special attention must be paid to areas with low levels of unionization and where the union is at risk of weakening. This involves increased efforts in industries characterized by low wages and insecure working conditions, and a strengthened effort to organize members with higher education. It is a goal for the union and the unions that young workers and students should choose to join a union. A common challenge for all unions is membership changes and attrition due to job changes. Many are not aware that they lose rights and insurance in such situations. The work to limit membership leakage must be a high priority for the unions. Experience from projects in several unions shows that systematic follow-up of these cases leads to a high rate of membership recovery. In some industries, there is intense competition for members. The consequences of changes in the labor market require strengthened cooperation between the unions in the union to increase the level of unionization and collective bargaining power. New and creative solutions must be developed in recruitment efforts. Initiatives are being taken to establish dialogue between the unions on how to develop the membership offerings and the content of membership.

3.2. Member services and member retention. Active local branches – a strong community that includes members in decision-making and achieves results through local negotiations – build loyalty and a sense of belonging to the labor movement. Therefore, the work to strengthen local branches is the best way to stop attrition and ensure the development of new representatives. Therefore, the union will support the work that the unions are doing by contributing to the development of methods and good strategies for establishing more such branches. Local efforts can be supported by digital solutions. These solutions should primarily target members who do not have a local branch in their workplace, and new members who are not initially integrated into the local branch. The union will be a partner when the unions are working to develop new digital solutions if the union desires cooperation. Digital solutions can never fully replace human contact and judgment. The best way to engage with members and potential members is to quickly connect with another person who can listen to what you have to say and help you find solutions. It will also be difficult to find digital solutions that place new members in the correct union. This requires skilled people with a lot of experience.

The union will assist affiliated organizations in developing methods and strategies to increase the number of active local chapters within companies. The union's organizational program aims to be a partner in developing new digital solutions that can support organizational work within affiliated organizations, when they desire this. It will also facilitate the development of skills in frontline services within affiliated organizations and contribute to making important information and training resources available in multiple languages.

3.  Organization in the service sector: The service sector accounts for approximately 3 out of every 10 jobs and a similar proportion of value creation in Norway. The service sector encompasses a range of industries characterized by significant challenges related to low wages, weak labor organization, and a high proportion of part-time employment. This is linked to low unionization rates and collective bargaining coverage. The service sector also has a significant proportion of foreign workers and ethnic minorities, making parts of the sector an important platform for inclusion. The union will collaborate with affiliated organizations to strengthen and continue the work of organizing within the service sector. Regional branches will develop and implement their own organizational initiatives as part of a common strategy. Joint training programs for employee representatives in the service sector will be conducted.

4.  Self-employed individuals: It is a natural extension of the union's commitment to work to improve the working conditions of self-employed individuals and freelancers. This is because many of these individuals have a similar dependence on their clients as employees have on their employers. Currently, there are over 330,000 self-employed individuals in Norway. Approximately 200,000 have self-employment as their primary or significant source of income. For some, this offers more freedom and flexibility, while for others, it brings insecurity and instability. The union cannot offer self-employed individuals collective bargaining agreements or local chapters with employee representatives. However, experience from affiliated organizations that already have self-employed members shows that various types of membership support is valuable to them. The union will further develop and strengthen cooperation with affiliated organizations and their membership offerings to meet the needs of self-employed individuals. It will also contribute to promoting the common political interests of affiliated organizations regarding business, social, and labor market policies related to self-employment. Membership offerings tailored to self-employed individuals will be developed in collaboration with affiliated organizations.

5.  Employees with higher education: The proportion of jobs requiring higher education is increasing. The union and affiliated organizations must work together to increase unionization rates within these professions. We must maintain and strengthen our position in the labor market and build the foundation for a fair and equitable collective bargaining policy. It is crucial for the union and affiliated organizations to collectively strengthen and modernize the organization to meet the changes in the labor market. Employees with higher education face the same challenges in the labor market as other workers. Graduates of universities and colleges need a strong labor movement. The union must therefore strengthen its efforts and demonstrate its commitment to this group. The union will organize annual conferences that address current issues in the labor market, which are particularly relevant to this group. It will also create platforms where members in the same field can meet. Through external activities, the union will demonstrate its relevance and attractiveness to members with higher education.

6.  The union's young members: The years spent in school, studying, and entering the workforce for the first time can be marked by significant changes. Most people are happy in their educational institutions and workplaces. However, many are unaware of their rights and obligations. It should be reassuring to know that you are a member of a union that protects your interests. The union aims to be the largest worker organization among young people up to the age of 35. A key priority for the union is to be present where young people are: in various educational institutions and learning environments. Young workers are positive about union membership. Early awareness-raising among school students, apprentices, and students through the union is crucial to ensure the organization of future workers. Young members are also in the workplace, and employee representatives must be aware of the need to engage young members and encourage more of them to take on roles in local chapters and the union. To successfully recruit more young people, it is important to assess whether the union and affiliated organizations are effectively reaching out to them and offering relevant membership benefits. During the congress period, the union will initiate collaborations with affiliated organizations to develop and modernize membership offerings for young members. The union is responsible for coordinating and following up on joint activities that affiliated organizations agree to implement.

Here are some examples of important joint activities, including the summer patrol, the union, and the apprentice patrol. The union has found it valuable to collaborate with the Labour Party to find political solutions. Therefore, it is important for the union to strengthen its political cooperation with the largest political youth organization, AUF, and other organizations, so that our young members can contribute to influencing political developments.

3.7 Youth Work: The union plays a central role in the union's work with young workers. The summer patrol protects the interests of young workers and highlights the importance of the union's work in creating a safe and good working life. Participation in the summer patrol provides important training and knowledge for future employee representatives. The union will further develop and strengthen the summer patrol. The goal is to increase participation in the patrol and to visit more companies. Apprentices are the future skilled workers. Apprentices are part of the regular working life and need the membership benefits and the sense of community that membership provides. The union will help apprentices and provide them with the tools that are important to ensure that their apprenticeship is as good as possible. The goal is to increase the number of apprentices who join the union's federation. The union will work with the federations to ensure that the apprentice patrol is carried out every year throughout the congress period. The union is the largest organization representing students within the union. It is important to maintain this as a social meeting place for young employee representatives, and it is a forum where young employee representatives and members of the union can be heard. The union has the opportunity to submit proposals and statements for consideration by the union. The union will conduct outreach and information activities for students, apprentices, and young workers, and facilitate cooperation and joint activities between the union and the union's federations. The union will further develop and strengthen youth work. The union and the federations have a joint responsibility to prioritize resources and ensure increased participation in the union and the union's activities. The union will further develop and strengthen the union's membership offerings for young members.

3.8 Student Work: The number of students and professionals with higher education has increased in the last 20 years. This group is a rapidly growing segment that will continue to be a significant part of the workforce in Norway. Studies by Fafo show that younger workers with higher education choose their organizational affiliation early on. Therefore, it is crucial for the success of student work and the future level of unionization that the federations cooperate and think long-term. More than half of the union members who join one of the union's federations during their studies continue to be members of a federation when they enter the workforce. This shows that the union must be visible at educational institutions. Union membership does not have the same educational or professional restrictions as in other major organizations. To ensure that we retain union members in the union's federations after their studies, the union, together with the federations, must develop uniform procedures to ensure that students are transferred to the correct federation when they start working after their studies. The union should be the largest organization among students and those with higher education. The union will offer students and student members professional courses and lectures, a social community, and bring the working life and student life closer together. Ensure that students receive help at the workplace from a union federation, regardless of where they work during their studies and which federation they are members of. The union should be run by and for the union and be linked to the educational institution. The union will create meeting places where student politics are on the agenda, for example, the union. In addition, it is necessary to facilitate cooperation and joint activities with other organizations that are involved in student politics. The goal for the congress period is that all federations should have the same membership benefits in the union concept, and the same membership fee. The union will further develop the union as a student organization at educational institutions throughout the country, develop the content of student membership, and facilitate cooperation with student organizations.
