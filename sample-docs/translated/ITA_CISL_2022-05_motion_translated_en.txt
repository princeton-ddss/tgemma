unione i the 2ESSE conference: "CHANGING TOGETHER" - People, Work, Participation for the Future of the Country. Rome, May 25-28, 2022. FINAL RESOLUTION: The commitment of a new Europe to combat the pandemic and the war at our doorstep. The unione conference, held in Rome from May 25 to 28, 2022, after hearing the report of the Secretary General, Luigi Sbarra, approved it with the contributions that emerged from the debate. The Congress is deeply concerned about the current international situation, which, as never before, challenges the supreme value of peace in the world. In this historical context, Europe is experiencing one of the most delicate moments in its history, and it must necessarily strengthen itself through a renewed commitment that places solidarity between peoples and guarantees of freedom at the center, advocating for a revision of the decision-making process, moving from a system of unanimity to a system of majority rule. In particular, it is necessary to continue supporting expansionary economic policies to support the States, which must not be limited to the emergency phase, but directed towards the search for structural solutions that go beyond the rigidity of the "stability pact," consolidating Next Generation EU, and as soon as possible, financing a new "Sure" plan to support employment and developing the strategies of the RePower EU initiative, which must be the first step towards a unified energy strategy. The renewed Union must strengthen its role by establishing high social standards and consolidating the role of work and cohesion in the European model, promoting the creation of a European tax system and new economic partnership agreements between the States, and working to contain the emergence and spread of populist and illiberal tendencies that have led to the current conflict situation. Furthermore, it is necessary to promote the involvement of member countries in a common, autonomous, and complementary defense, alongside the broader Atlantic Alliance, to foster a healthy, positive, and active role in support of the indispensable values of democracy and coexistence among peoples, and to revive the principles of a peace based on human rights, which must be built with concrete actions that go beyond mere solidarity. The 19th Congress, reaffirming values such as justice, freedom of thought, and social cohesion, calls on the Organization to continue its work in supporting the Ukrainian community in the ways and forms implemented by the Confederation, and calls on the international community and Europe to make every diplomatic effort for an immediate ceasefire and a peace plan. Reaffirming its commitment to European integration, the 19th Congress of the unione believes it is essential to reform the institutional structure of the European Union to achieve the goal of a united Europe, aware that its fulfillment also pursues the objective of a strong and solidary Italy playing a leading role within the Union. Similarly, the process of integrating the European representation of trade unions and employers' organizations, and strengthening the X, must be pursued in order to further influence the policies of the Union, implement the European Social Pillar, and strengthen our action in European Works Councils, supporting transnational bargaining by emphasizing the portability of social rights within the Union and promoting convergence measures in the healthcare sector. The 19th Congress of the unione hopes that, given the delicate economic situation in the country, political parties and institutions will promote a strong space for social dialogue, overcoming any resistance to change. In particular, the 19th Congress believes that the right path is to implement a policy based on authentic values that places the active participation of workers' and employers' representatives at the center, to support growth, development, and social cohesion. "Being there to change": We are aware of the role that trade unions must play in supporting the processes of transition, which is why the motto of the Confederation Congress is "being there to change," because the transformations in the country must have a greater impact on people and work, and this requires strong involvement of the trade unions in the fundamental decisions for our nation. In this sense, the Confederation Congress believes it is essential to support the implementation of the projects of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) in coordination with other sources of funding, which are crucial for addressing the issues of economic recovery and employment, with particular attention to the areas in greatest need, especially the Mezzogiorno.

The organization recognizes the Draghi government's merit in ending the disastrous period of disintermediation, initiating a new phase of social dialogue. The Congress hopes this dialogue will evolve into a renewed and structural era of strong cooperation, fostering a new framework that strengthens social cohesion, supports the interests of workers, and enhances the competitiveness of the Italian system. The outcome of this process must be an urgent and renewed Social Pact that reconnects the economy, society, and labor, rebuilding a sense of hope in the country, echoing the words of the President of the Republic, Sergio Mattarella, who has repeatedly advocated for a productive season of cooperation on shared and essential goals. The pandemic has exacerbated social, economic, and geographical inequalities, often leading to exclusion. This presents the challenge for unions and policymakers to seek a new dimension of common work. Therefore, the 19th Congress commits the organization to building a society based on the recognition of fair and sustainable work, valuing the contribution of everyone, promoting solidarity that leaves no one behind, and channeling any signs of discouragement or resentment into a project capable of countering negative behaviors and sentiments. Aware that the rule of law in an unequal society is the cornerstone for the equality of citizens, the 19th Congress supports the organization's stance on "work and legality," believing that justice, employment, and development are strongly interconnected and require "local agreements" for legality and cohesion, aimed at preventing any form of criminal infiltration in territorial development processes and the exploitation of labor. In an increasingly internationalized criminal context, the Congress also supports the proposal to extend the "La Torre" law within the European community. The season of change we are experiencing encourages us to live according to Pope Francis's call to never lose sight of true priorities, marking the peripheries with our social commitment and rejecting the "culture of waste." In this regard, union action must continue to be guided by the recovery of fairness, based on the principle of redistribution to reduce the disadvantages faced by workers and retirees through a renewed power of collective bargaining. Furthermore, recognizing that the level of protection for work and income represents a lifeline for workers and retirees, the organization must continue to pursue the goal of a major tripartite agreement involving unions, businesses, and the government, placing development and "wage policy" at the center of the discussion. The objective is to recreate, after the experience of the pandemic, a more cohesive and equitable society, implementing innovative contractual and fiscal measures to realign wages and pensions with inflation, supporting the most affected categories and income levels, and promoting real wage growth by recognizing incremental improvements in relation to productivity and competitiveness, while pursuing adequate goals for fair and sustainable well-being.

The Challenges of the Present: The 19th Congress expresses serious concern about the significant repercussions of the pandemic crisis, which persist to this day. Despite the union's efforts to establish safety protocols that have boosted the vaccination campaign, and our proactive stance in favor of mandatory vaccination, these challenges continue to affect the entire national community, manifesting as:

*   A failure to recover employment levels to pre-pandemic levels. The union is concerned about the increase in precarious jobs, which do not address the underlying issues of unemployment and NEETs (Not in Education, Employment, or Training). Our organization aims not for precarious full employment, but for stable, quality employment with proper protections, training, and contracts, including new safeguards for various forms of work.
*   Difficulties in entire production sectors, particularly those involved in ecological transitions. There is a need for a national industrial policy that revitalizes the potential and attractiveness of the manufacturing sector. Further support measures are needed, starting with agriculture and food production, construction, industry, and services, as well as trade and tourism, which are still struggling to recover.
*   An increase in contractual dumping, underemployment, and involuntary part-time work, exacerbated by the economic crisis following the pandemic, particularly in the tertiary and service sectors. This has not only eroded wages but has also led to increased poverty among workers and pensioners. The 19th Congress prioritizes the renewal of all public and private contracts, redefining mechanisms for managing wage dynamics.
*   Significant problems caused by worrying inflationary pressures, which, despite measures to support families, young people, and pensioners, are expanding the range of people affected by absolute and relative poverty.
*   Delays in measures to support the public sector, which has historically played a crucial role in national development. Despite the important "Pacts for Innovation and Education" signed between the government and the unions, and strongly advocated by the union, there is still a need to overcome the disinvestment experienced in recent years, which has led to a decline in public services and a dangerous reduction in employment. The union, enriched by the excellent results of recent public trade union elections, where the organization has excelled in all sectors, must urgently continue to pursue the goal of reforming the public administration with the involvement of the unions, to provide the country with an administration that is increasingly responsive to the needs of citizens and businesses, through a strong program of recruitment, training, and professional development, enabling workers to meet the challenges of innovation, while overcoming the constraints of public finances and legislation that often hinder collective bargaining. A public administration that is less burdened by bureaucracy and procedures, and more focused on ensuring quality and efficiency in service delivery, promoting union participation in the organization of work. The centrality of the knowledge, skills, and competencies chain must be reaffirmed, as it is closely linked to the employability of young people. There is a clear contradiction between the content of the agreements signed between the government and the unions and the provisions of the recent decree 36/2022, which must be necessarily revised without encroaching on the legal aspects of contractual matters, to protect the autonomy of educational institutions, including in terms of identifying training needs to improve the quality of education, and defining appropriate methods for recruiting staff. This is in the exclusive interest of the educational mission, which should never be reduced to market logic, to ensure full access to the right to education, strengthening measures to combat school dropout and the NEET phenomenon.

Capitalizing on the development opportunities offered by the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR). Through the exercise of a participatory governance function for the measures aimed at economic recovery outlined in the PNRR, the organization must continue to:

*   Monitor the expenditure of community resources, which constitute the primary source of funding for initiatives promoting development, growth, and employment.
*   Maintain a collaborative relationship with the government, initiated with the signing of the Protocol for the implementation of the PNRR, extending this collaboration at the national, regional, and sectoral levels to ensure compliance with social conditions that must accompany projects and reforms, and to verify the effective use of available resources, with particular attention to the allocation of 40% of these resources to the southern regions.
*   Strengthen institutional dialogue on active labor market policies and new skills, which currently appear to be underperforming in terms of impact and results. This should facilitate the strengthening of protections and income support for those most vulnerable, a group that has significantly increased due to the pandemic and the current economic situation, as well as the impact of technological and digital advancements on the labor market. This includes ensuring the creation of a "skills passport" for each individual and the right to be protected during career transitions.

In this context, the role of employment services must be strengthened, with concrete implementation of the GOL program. This program must foster strong synergy between public and private accredited entities, the National Employment Agency (ANPAL), and the productive system. It should address the challenge of creating an attractive labor market, especially for young people, combating the phenomenon of "brain drain," the depopulation of vulnerable areas, exploitative labor practices, and undeclared work. To address these issues, inspection services must be strengthened, and penalties for non-compliant employers must be increased, including the possibility of imprisonment.

Regarding support for younger generations, the 19th Congress commits the organization to continuing on the established path, further strengthening actions to support the presence of increasingly skilled and motivated young people in the labor market, and encouraging their involvement in trade union structures.

The organization must also support the need for a strong commitment to lifelong learning, ensuring that all workers, through collective bargaining, have access to appropriate training, digital literacy, and the right to retraining. This will expand the number of adults enrolled in continuous learning and training programs, both in relation to their career path and through the strengthening of vocational training schools (ITS) and the role of the university and research system, which are drivers of societal development. This should be achieved by strengthening connections with business networks.

Furthermore, the organization should promote a comprehensive regeneration of the manufacturing sector, based on a renewed industrial vision that supports transformation and revitalizes the country's key strategic assets. This should address environmental transitions in a socially sustainable manner, continue dialogue, seek solutions to corporate crises, and establish rules to combat relocation and international speculation, with the aim of shortening global value chains, also in response to difficulties in sourcing raw materials and the increasing cost of semi-finished products.

In addition, policies should be implemented to support businesses through a regulatory framework that can provide new impetus to entrepreneurial initiatives that address the innovative challenges of sustainability and "Industry 4.0." This should be complemented by a new "Work 4.0" approach, in which to invest, to more easily address major technological, industrial, energy, environmental, and demographic transitions, ensuring that they are fair and inclusive, and therefore contribute to improving the quality and quantity of work.

The project to establish a community authority for assessing the social sustainability of businesses should be promoted. Policies should be strengthened to support sectors that, for evident reasons of potential and despite the effects of the pandemic, can represent an important source of employment in all regions of the country, with particular attention to agriculture, construction, crafts, tourism, commerce, and more broadly, the service sector. This should involve linking the accessibility of the landscape/cultural heritage and the promotion of local products to the creation of businesses, with a specific focus on youth and women.  Responses must be provided to families, workers, and retirees, reconsidering the role of healthcare and social welfare as investments to support the country's growth. In this direction, the Congress calls on the Confederation Secretariat to support the measures and actions planned in the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) to overcome the current weaknesses of the healthcare system, starting with staffing levels. This should involve seeking a renewed balance between prevention, care, and rehabilitation, and promoting a comprehensive social and welfare system, also through strengthening and defining a new governance structure for local social services to respond to the new needs of the national community, particularly in relation to the aging population and the resulting increase in chronic diseases. This necessitates a comprehensive law on non-self-sufficiency, providing a strong impetus for social dialogue. It is even more crucial to identify a "social clause" for the application of national collective bargaining agreements signed by the most representative trade unions within the systems of accreditation and awarding of healthcare services, social, and welfare services, to preserve the universality and levels of performance of the national healthcare system. This condition should be extended to all public service contracts.  Furthermore, the principles of universality of the National Healthcare Service should be upheld, along with social policies aimed at guaranteeing uniform essential levels throughout the national territory, with the hope of revising Title V of the Constitution to overcome ambiguities and uncertainties in the distribution of powers between the State and the Regions, with the aim of strengthening the relationship between the different areas of the country, with reference to the recovery and promotion of inland and mountain areas, small islands, and the protection and revitalization of smaller municipalities, even in association.  The problem of infrastructure development in the country should be addressed in a modern, digital, sustainable, and interconnected manner, through the implementation of major projects and smaller-scale projects that are useful for the local economy, providing a structural solution to the transportation system, the mobility of people and goods. Particular attention should be paid to monitoring investments identified in the PNRR for digital infrastructure, roads, railway networks, airports, ports, and interventions related to soil protection from erosion and degradation, waste management through support for circular economy processes, which also include the use of waste-to-energy technologies, and addressing the water scarcity issues, especially in the southern regions.  Our Commitment: The Congress, sharing the political and trade union orientations represented in the report of the Secretariat, believes it is necessary to go beyond the ordinary policies implemented in recent years, which are often based on a defensive strategy of preserving the status quo. It is pursuing several major objectives for which it is calling on the Confederation Secretariat, including:  Providing recognition and value to good work that has saved the country in the last two years. This means promoting productive, stable work that is focused on building a fulfilling life for people, free from discrimination, and well-protected, even as it evolves. This also involves addressing and monitoring the transparency of algorithms used in platform-based work, and, above all, ensuring safety, given the data on accidents and, unfortunately, the too many deaths at work, which are unacceptable in a civilized country. In this sense, the Congress calls on the Organization to continue to maintain a high level of mobilization as a priority for the union, further strengthening prevention measures, social protections, and the system of controls, and using protective measures such as a "point-based license" and "company qualification," which should be linked to contracts. All INAIL (National Institute for Insurance against Accidents at Work) resources should be fully invested with the goal of achieving zero workplace accidents.

Key areas to be addressed in the discussion to achieve a "Statute of the Individual in the Labor Market," for which the 19th Congress commits the Confederation to protecting and supporting every individual during transitions between school and work, between jobs, between work and family life, and in managing care responsibilities, using income support tools linked to retraining and job placement programs. This includes: promoting greater integration between education, training, and work, making dual education systems the norm for young people and strengthening apprenticeship programs; strengthening measures to support female employment by promoting a widespread network of welfare and support services, starting with childcare facilities, and negotiating flexible working hours to accommodate care responsibilities, ensuring equal pay; encouraging the adoption of formalized flexible work arrangements, both as a work-life balance solution for employees and as a driver of national development. Promoting the quality of female employment also requires a strong commitment to combating workplace harassment and gender-based violence. It is crucial to revitalize collective bargaining as the primary tool for managing changes in the workplace and protecting workers, consolidating a plural and innovative bargaining system, strengthening bilateral negotiations, and expanding the two levels of bargaining, which are increasingly integrated, while giving great importance to the second level, which includes company, territorial, and network-based bargaining. The main objective of collective bargaining in this context is to contribute to a new income policy that increases the value of wages and salaries, pursuing a model of "universal contractual welfare" capable of increasing the productivity of businesses and redistributing it to workers, while also supporting regulatory interventions with provisions for greater tax and contribution benefits. Bargaining should also address the new needs for sustainable work, which are increasingly important to workers, through a more comprehensive contractual welfare system, a policy of flexible working hours, guaranteed professional development, balanced remote work, and recognizing the growing contribution of workers to company results. A significant effort must be made to protect citizens and businesses from rising prices, particularly "energy costs," through maximum cost thresholds and by increasing the use of national natural resources, developing renewable energy sources, facilitating the construction of regasification plants, and strengthening supply networks. Particular attention should be paid to trends in credit dynamics, restoring the role of credit as a driver of national entrepreneurship and revitalizing the active role of Cassa Depositi e Prestiti in industrial policies and investment attraction processes. A comprehensive tax reform is needed, one that is truly progressive and redistributive, relieving the burden on low and middle-income earners from work and pensions, strengthening the fight against tax evasion, imposing an additional contribution on speculative income and large multinational corporations in the digital economy, for which the pursuit of profit has often taken precedence over the well-being of the community. In particular, salary components derived from the second level of bargaining, both in the private and public sectors, and negotiated welfare measures should be tax-exempt. The pension system must be reformed to make it more inclusive, equitable, and flexible, taking into account the heterogeneity of jobs, periods of unemployment, care work, and the shortcomings of the female and youth labor markets.

The following measures will be implemented:

*   Strengthening social security for women, supporting supplementary pension schemes through both fiscal incentives and increased enrollment through opt-in mechanisms, and guaranteeing the purchasing power of existing pension benefits. The Union will also work to reform the governance structures of social security and insurance institutions through the participation of social partners in the boards of directors.
*   Promoting a modern approach to solidarity and volunteering, and fostering the involvement of older adults to ensure active aging. The Union will work to bridge the generational gap, recognizing the role of older adults in families and their contribution to a new model of society where young people are "protagonists of work."
*   Addressing the historical divide between the North and South of the country to promote national growth, emphasizing the need for shared implementation of reforms and projects outlined in the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (PNRR) at all levels. The Union will commit to supporting the economic development of the South and overcoming the "Southern Question."
*   Implementing an inclusive policy that values the presence of migrant citizens in the country, with a particular focus on the second generation, facilitating renewed integration measures, promoting citizenship through jus soli and jus culturae, and fostering interculturalism as a driver of innovation in society and the workplace.
*   Identifying new forms of collaboration and synergy with the Third Sector, recognizing its activities of general interest with civic, solidarity, and social utility goals.
*   Continuing to support economic growth by stimulating domestic demand and consumption, promoting employment, and introducing assistance programs to facilitate purchases for families, while also increasing measures to combat high rental costs, revitalize public housing, and protect vulnerable social groups.
*   The 19th Congress calls on the Union to engage with employers and industry representatives to promote a modern model of industrial relations based on responsibility and active participation at all levels of negotiation.

A new era of participation and collective bargaining:

The 19th Congress reaffirms that collective bargaining is the primary tool for finding free and creative, generative, and adaptive solutions that address new needs both inside and outside the workplace. The flexibility needed to support the social and economic system during times of great uncertainty should be sought through negotiation, tailored to the specific needs of different work communities, sectors, and individuals. In this regard, the Congress emphasizes that negotiated and regulated flexibility, as defined in collective agreements, such as in temporary employment and temporary work agencies, should not be confused with precariousness, and must guarantee protections and equal treatment.

At the same time, the 19th Congress rejects proposals from certain political forces that do not meet the expectations of workers, undermining the autonomy of collective bargaining and social partners, particularly regarding minimum wages and legally regulated representation. Instead, it reiterates the need to combat unfair labor practices, giving legal recognition to wages established in national collective bargaining agreements signed by representative organizations and widely applied in relevant sectors, measured according to effective and shared criteria.

It is also essential to bring to the attention of institutions the need to open dialogue on the implementation of Article 46 of the Constitution, which concerns participation, supporting organizational participation models and co-management of companies as a strategic vision that includes the presence of workers in the oversight and management of companies, and forms of economic democracy through employee financial and equity participation.

These issues highlight the need to define innovative social and industrial relations and to strengthen the system of bilateral agreements as a safeguard for collective bargaining and interprofessional and parity funds, encouraging pension funds to promote economic growth through investment in the real economy.

In particular, the 19th Congress of the Union mandates the Confederation to initiate procedures to support a popular legislative proposal on participation, also promoting the participation of public sector employees in the organizational decisions of administrations.

The network of services for individuals. The Congress also calls for further efforts to strengthen the synergistic operational capacity of the service system, including: the union, the CAF (social security assistance center), the labor office, and other essential components of the organization, which are crucial social presences in the territory. The Congress believes it is essential to further develop this important network of expertise, which every day re-establishes connections with the real world, directly addressing the needs and expectations of millions of people. This system must be increasingly tailored to the needs of members and representatives, and must continue to be coordinated through a network approach, with strong cooperation with the federations, in the spirit of a practical confederation, demonstrating a strong commitment to promoting membership and ensuring continuity of association, with the aim of expanding the membership base and preventing the loss of valuable experience during career transitions, ultimately strengthening representation through investment in communication and information in the workplace, which will promote the culture and identity of the union. Therefore, the Congress urges the Executive Committee to plan, with timelines and methods to be determined, an organizational assembly aimed at updating and strengthening the range of services offered by the union. Similarly, the union's role in the international trade union landscape must be strengthened, including through the reinforcement of the relevant department and by continuing to provide concrete contributions to the growth of the union at the European level. In particular, trade union training must be further consolidated in synergy with the confederation departments, as a political and organizational expression of the union, which will strengthen the cultural and contractual proposals and involve an increasing number of young people, women, and migrants in the association's structures: the future trade unionists of the 21st century. To this end, the role of the union and its affiliated organizations must be further developed. The Congress condemns and rejects any attempts to undermine the role of social intermediary services through actions that increase workloads and responsibilities while simultaneously reducing the resources allocated to them. Conclusions. Finally, the Congress reaffirms that the union of today will continue to draw its roots from the prophetic legacy of the thought of Pastore and Romani, and accepts the challenge set by the General Secretary, Luigi Sbarra, of an organization that is "committed to leading, not following," and that is determined to courageously implement a plan for change in the coming years, based on an ideal and organizational drive to further strengthen the values of unity, work, people, and social inclusion.
