Statement of Principles: In response to the social, economic, and advocacy challenges of the 21st century, which differ from those of the past, and to increase the effectiveness of trade union work in protecting the interests of workers, the trade union is establishing a federation. This federation will bring together the largest number of organized workers in the current Hungarian trade union movement. The new organization, and the complete unification of the three founding organizations, represents not only responsibility but also a serious commitment. Our primary commitment is to represent the interests of trade union members, the entire workforce, including those in the public and private sectors, civil servants, public employees, members of the armed forces and law enforcement agencies, those who have lost their jobs, people with disabilities, students, and retirees, as well as to strengthen civil society and a democratic Hungary. Our union is based on fundamental values such as solidarity, equality of opportunity, democracy, patriotism, Europeanism, the rule of law, and political neutrality. The three founding confederations and their member organizations believe in and are committed to upholding these values. We believe that only by adhering to these values can we build a modern Hungary in the 21st century. Our federation's policies will take into account the common values and goals of the democratic countries that make up the European Union, while respecting our national historical traditions. We want a Hungary that, within a framework of civil democratic relations, respects private property and freedom of enterprise, and is capable of establishing and maintaining a social and labor dialogue—where necessary—through legislation, so that workers and the broadest segments of society—regardless of their current situation—can benefit from the results achieved. Therefore, the trade union wants to create a qualitatively different relationship with the branches of government, the government, employers, and civil society in Hungary. Our size and social embeddedness entitle us to do so. We believe that we are not alone in the world, and we are reaching out to partners for cooperation, and through our member organizations, we are ready to continuously expand our network and engage with new social groups. The trade union federation we have created is based on the principle of voluntarism. This must apply to the world of work and in the work of our member organizations. To this end, the trade union will establish internal cooperation that respects the freedom of organization and the autonomy of member organizations and sectors, ensuring effective advocacy. We believe that the interests of our members are clearly reflected in the sectoral organizations of the advocacy system. The trade union's task is to support the work of these sectoral organizations. The trade union represents the interests of workers in the public and private sectors without discrimination. We want to work more effectively, harmonizing and amplifying the will and intentions of the two major groups, and thereby representing their interests. In this way, we will be able to coordinate the representation of common interests in the existing forums for dialogue and achieve effective and broad internal cooperation. At the same time, we believe that it is essential to establish a comprehensive social dialogue at the macro level. We intend to negotiate with all employer and employee advocacy bodies and the government, taking into account the jointly defined criteria for representativeness, to achieve this. The trade union seeks to have a strong and influential presence in the Hungarian advocacy system. To this end, it will provide member organizations with effective expert support and a visible local, regional, and international presence, thereby serving the interests of their members, the workers of Hungary. We want to achieve together that the trade union actively serves the needs of its member organizations, and that, in doing so, the member organizations can directly demonstrate the benefits and effectiveness of the new organization to their own members. To this end, the trade union will establish a unified education and training system, as well as a trade union research center that is essential for the professional and high-quality operation of the federation. The trade union movement has never been hostile to those who have not yet joined its ranks, because we fundamentally believe in the idea of solidarity. Therefore, we must also help those who are unable or unwilling to join us. We must create opportunities for them to join the trade union, opportunities that, if necessary, will protect them from employer retaliation.

In order to reach out to those who may not yet share our goals, we need to demonstrate the core of our advocacy work and how we can help workers. The primary task of the trade union is to support the growth of its membership. We need to convince more people to join us, to embrace the community we offer, and to become members, even if their employer doesn't look favorably upon it. Breaking down the walls of vulnerability begins where a helping hand is offered and accepted, and every worker should feel, and know, that they are not alone. To this end, the federation is renewing its communication tools and methods. The trade union is open to all workers' advocacy organizations and unions. It welcomes all trade unions in Hungary to join its ranks. We are ready to cooperate with any trade union. Our advocacy community is created not for division, but to increase trade union and, through that, social solidarity. In its advocacy and representation activities, the trade union is ready to enter into agreements with employer organizations, the current government, and all legitimate actors in society, including those that involve mutually beneficial compromises. We are capable of cooperating with all political and social actors who accept the rules of democracy and do not discriminate against others. Today's Hungary has lost its momentum. Economic growth and social progress have stalled. Due to the mistakes of the past decades, values that once made us a leading player in the region have been lost. Social solidarity has declined, and vulnerability has increased. We want a Hungary where the elimination of exploitation is a social minimum. A country can only be strong and capable of development if mutual care and a willingness to act in the common interest are found in even the smallest communities. We want a Hungary where communities are not torn apart by power, but are supported and fostered, taking into account the diversity of society, because without communities, the society's ability to protect itself is at risk. The largest of the remaining and solidarity-based communities is the trade union movement. Therefore, we have a great responsibility, and with it, a duty, to revive the society's immune system, and to find partners who share our values and work with us. We want a Hungary where it is not a privilege for workers to benefit from economic growth. We know that progress cannot be achieved without working people. This requires respect for workers, not their humiliation. It is not possible to build a strong country if politics artificially divides it. It is unacceptable to pit the economy against the public sector, to create an imbalance in incomes, to have a state withdrawal system that favors the rich, and to have a social welfare system that drives the poor into poverty, demanding but giving unfairly and disproportionately. We want a Hungary where we jointly plan our future and work together to achieve it. Where the future of our grandchildren is not written by political opportunists or temporary adventurers. We know and feel the responsibility to act. The trade union has the intellectual background and experience to participate in the social debate on the most important issues that affect us—and we believe, the entire society: raising incomes, eliminating working poverty; reforming the unsustainable pension system, creating its long-term predictability, and improving the social situation of retirees; promoting the social inclusion of those in difficult situations; providing fair assistance to those who have been unfairly excluded from the labor market; establishing real rights for workers, not just in laws; creating a political and economic system necessary for economic growth and job creation; developing an educational and vocational training system that supports development and the future of young people; creating a social and healthcare system that operates taking into account both opportunities and needs; and creating a sustainable development for the country. We consider it our task to ensure that parties focus on what is important for Hungary, and not on acquiring or maintaining power. We believe that the trade union, as the largest trade union organization in Hungary, is capable of opening a new chapter for the Hungarian trade union movement in the 21st century.

The trade union organization assumes the intellectual legacy of the more than one and a half centuries of the Hungarian trade union movement, including the values that represent a shared treasure of the independent histories of its three founding organizations – the trade union, the trade union, and the trade union – and their previous work in protecting and representing the interests of workers. The trade union organization that is creating the new federation does not consider the accepted documents, including this program statement, as a final step, but rather as the starting point for the realization of a modern and unified workers' interest organization, which we believe is essential for the development of modern Hungary. SUMMARY: UNITY - SOLIDARITY.
