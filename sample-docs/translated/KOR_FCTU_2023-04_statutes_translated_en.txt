Article 1 (Name) The name of this organization shall be the Labor Union, and the abbreviation shall be the Labor Union. The English name shall be the Labor Union, and the English abbreviation shall be the Labor Union.
Article 2 (Office) The principal office of the Labor Union shall be located in Seoul.
Article 3 (Legal Entity) The Labor Union may be established as a legal entity.
Article 4 (Purpose and Activities) The purpose of the Labor Union is to improve the political, economic, and social status of workers and to enhance the quality of life for all citizens, and to build a unified nation and a democratic society where human dignity and equality are guaranteed. To achieve this, the Labor Union shall carry out the following activities:
1. Strengthening the political power of workers and solidarity with democratic forces.
2. Establishing national independence, securing democratic rights, and peacefully unifying the divided nation.
3. Organizing unorganized and non-regular workers to strengthen organizational capabilities.
4. Establishing a system of joint bargaining and joint struggles by industry, building industry-specific unions, and unifying the entire labor movement.
5. Crushing the oppression and control of power and capital, and fully securing workers' basic rights such as the right of teachers and public officials to organize.
6. Expanding participation in management based on collective decision-making and eliminating undemocratic elements in the workplace.
7. Improving working conditions such as securing a living wage, guaranteeing job security, reducing working hours, and expanding maternity protection.
8. Eliminating all forms of discrimination and securing a safe and pleasant working environment.
9. Strengthening regulations on monopolies and protecting small and medium-sized enterprises and agriculture.
10. Reforming social security, housing, education, healthcare, taxation, finance, prices, banking, land, environment, and transportation.
11. Promoting international solidarity, human rights, and world peace.
12. Educational, propaganda, and publishing activities to achieve the above purposes and activities.
13. Other activities necessary to achieve the purposes of the Labor Union.

Chapter 2: Organization
Article 5 (Composition)
(1) The Labor Union shall be composed of industry-specific labor unions that agree with the declaration, charter, and articles of the Labor Union and whose membership is approved in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(2) Industry-specific labor unions refer to industry-specific labor unions and confederations at the national level, and national-level industry councils, occupational labor unions, and general labor unions that are equivalent to these shall be considered as affiliated units.
Article 6 (Regional Branches)
(1) To activate activities in each region in accordance with the declaration, charter, and various policies, the Labor Union shall establish regional branches as affiliated organizations, based on the metropolitan and provincial level, and may establish additional branches as needed.
(2) Regional branches shall be composed of unit labor unions or labor unions (labor unions, labor unions) in the respective region.
(3) The organization and operation of regional branches shall be governed by separate regulations.
Article 7 (Committees)
(1) The Labor Union may establish committees as needed to promote exchange and cooperation, and joint action among industry-specific labor unions of the same or related industries.
(2) The organization and operation of committees shall be governed by separate regulations.
Article 8 (Membership and Withdrawal)
(1) An industry-specific labor union that wishes to become a member of the Labor Union shall submit a prescribed application to the Labor Union and be approved by a vote of the Labor Union.
(2) An affiliated organization that wishes to withdraw from the Labor Union shall follow the prescribed procedures specified in the articles of that organization, attach a record of the meeting, and submit a withdrawal letter to the Labor Union, which shall be approved by a vote of the Labor Union.

Section 5: The Union Committee
Article 29 (Composition and Convening)
The Union Committee consists of the chairperson, the vice chairperson, the deputy chairperson, the secretary-general, the chairperson of the committee, the deputy secretary-general, the director, and the union chairperson. It is convened at least twice a month, and when the chairperson deems it necessary.

Article 30 (Functions)
The functions of the Union Committee are as follows:
1.  Executing the tasks entrusted to the union.
2.  Preparing for union meetings and drafting agenda items.
3.  Reviewing and executing various daily operations.
4.  Other necessary matters.

Section 6: The Union Committee
Article 31 (Composition and Convening)
(1) The Union Committee consists of a certain number of auditors elected by the union. They audit the financial transactions at least twice a year and report the results to the chairperson and the union.
(2) The Union Committee shall conduct an audit without delay if requested by one-third or more of the delegates or one-third or more of the central committee members.

Section 7: The Election Committee
Article 32 (Composition and Convening)
(1) An Election Committee may be established in accordance with the decision of the union.
(2) The chairperson appoints and dismisses the chairperson of the Election Committee, subject to the approval of the union.
(3) Matters necessary for the operation of each Election Committee shall be governed by separate regulations.

Section 8: The Election Committee
Article 33 (Composition and Convening)
(1) The Election Committee consists of no more than 15 members.
(2) Candidate members of the Election Committee are recommended by affiliated organizations and elected by the union.
(3) The chairperson and the secretary of the central election committee are elected from among the members.
(4) The term of office of the members of the Election Committee is 3 years, and the term of office of the substitute members is the remaining term.
(5) Matters necessary for the organization, operation, and election management of the Election Committee shall be governed by the Election Management Regulations.

Article 34 (Powers and Functions)
(1) The powers and functions of the Election Committee are as follows:
1.  Fair management of elections and member votes.
2.  Interpretation of the articles, regulations, and rules related to elections.
3.  Establishment and revision of the Election Management Regulations.
4.  Mediation and arbitration of election-related disputes.
5.  Guidance and supervision of the election management committees of affiliated organizations.
(2) Other functions, powers, and duties of the Election Committee, as well as other necessary matters not specified in paragraph 1, shall be governed by the Election Management Regulations.

Section 9: The Disciplinary Committee
Article 35 (Composition and Convening)
(1) The Disciplinary Committee consists of no more than 7 members selected from among the candidates recommended by the union and elected by the union.
(2) The chairperson of the Disciplinary Committee is elected from among the members.
(3) The term of office of the members of the Disciplinary Committee is 2 years, and they may be re-elected.
(4) The Disciplinary Committee holds regular meetings at least once per quarter, and the chairperson of the Disciplinary Committee may convene meetings as needed.

Article 36 (Functions)
The Disciplinary Committee handles cases where members violate the union's rules and regulations.

Chapter 6: The Executive Committee
Article 48 (Executive Committee)
An Executive Committee is established to execute the various tasks of the union. The Executive Committee includes the union executives and the full-time staff of affiliated unions.

Article 49 (Regulations regarding the Executive Committee)
Matters concerning the organization, operation, rules, personnel, and compensation of the Executive Committee shall be governed by separate regulations.

Chapter 7: The Branch Committee
Article 50 (Branch Committee)
(1) The union may establish branch committees, such as research institutes and training centers, in accordance with the decision of the union. However, the establishment, operation, and management thereof shall be governed by separate regulations.
(2) The chairperson appoints and dismisses the chairperson of the branch committee, subject to the approval of the union.
(3) The appointment and dismissal of members of the branch committee are made by the chairperson upon the recommendation of the chairperson.
(4) The provisions regarding the organization, operation, rules, personnel, and compensation of the branch committee apply mutatis mutandis to the members of the branch committee.

Chapter 8: Finance
Article 51 (Finance)
(1) The finances of the union are funded by mandatory contributions such as membership fees, funds, and levies paid by affiliated organizations, as well as other income. The funds are operated in accordance with the budget established by the union.
(2) If it is deemed that the operation of the union's finances cannot be carried out as planned due to unforeseen circumstances, it may be amended by the decision of the union.

Article 52 (Mandatory Contributions)
The types of mandatory contributions that affiliated organizations pay to the union are as follows:
1.  Membership Fee: An amount proportional to the number of member organizations reported by each affiliated organization (revised on 2023.04.24).
2.  Fund: An allocated amount for a specific project decided by the union.
3.  Levy: An amount specifically levied in addition to the membership fee and fund.

Article 53 (Membership Fee)
(1) The membership fee per member is a certain amount determined by the union.

Chapter 9 Awards and Disciplinary Actions
Article 59 (Awards) The chairperson may award organizations and members, and other individuals who have made significant contributions to the labor movement and the development of the labor union, with the approval of the labor union.
Article 60 (Restriction of Rights)
(1) If an affiliated organization fails to pay membership fees for more than three months, the rights of that organization, its representatives, and its members may be partially or fully restricted. (Revised on April 24, 2023)
(2) If a unit organization fails to pay membership fees for more than three months, the rights of that organization, its representatives, and its members may be partially or fully restricted. (Added on April 24, 2023)
(3) If a member fails to pay membership fees to a unit organization or an affiliated organization, the rights of that member may be partially or fully restricted. (Revised on April 24, 2023)
(4) The specific details of the restriction of rights or the reservation of the restriction of rights shall be specified in separate regulations. (Added on April 24, 2023)
Article 61 (Disciplinary Actions)
(1) The types of disciplinary actions include warnings, salary reductions, suspension from duty, dismissal, expulsion, and termination of membership, and the details shall be specified in the disciplinary regulations.
(2) The labor union may impose disciplinary actions in accordance with the provisions of the disciplinary regulations on affiliated organizations, subordinate organizations, executives, labor union members, and members who have committed the following acts:
1. Violation of the labor union's constitution, bylaws, resolutions, and regulations without justifiable reasons.
2. Disrupting the organization of the labor union or damaging its reputation.
3. Causing property damage to the labor union (executives, labor union members, and members).
4. Negligence of duty, unauthorized absence, violation of the principle of maintaining the overall operation (executives, labor union members).
5. Failure to pay membership fees for more than three months without justifiable reasons (affiliated organizations and subordinate organizations).
Article 62 (Disciplinary Regulations) The details regarding awards and disciplinary actions shall be specified in separate regulations.
Chapter 10 Dissolution
Article 63 (Dissolution) The labor union shall be dissolved when all affiliated organizations withdraw or when a special resolution as stipulated in Article 39 is passed.
Article 64 (Dissolution Procedures) When the dissolution of the labor union is resolved, the general assembly that resolved the dissolution shall determine the dissolution procedures and the liquidator.
Article 65 (General Principles) Matters not stipulated in this Articles of Association shall be governed by general principles.
