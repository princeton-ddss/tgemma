Corona has shown us how important social cohesion, comprehensive public services, and an effective state are. We have all experienced firsthand how valuable solidarity is and that this crisis can only be overcome by working together. We as a union reacted quickly – we focused on the health of employees, shaped the new union and played a key role in determining occupational health and safety. We succeeded in getting short-time work benefits increased and extended, and in finally sparking a broad debate in our country about the systemic relevance and value of work. We ensured that parents who had to stay at home and look after their children during the homeschooling period did not suffer massive financial and professional disadvantages – and we demonstrated during the crisis that gender equality is a central element of our union work. We have repeatedly made it clear to politicians that our model of co-determination, collective bargaining agreements, and strong unions is a reliable anchor for the people in our country, especially in times of crisis.
