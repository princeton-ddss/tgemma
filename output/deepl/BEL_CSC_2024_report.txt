We have fought for recognized, quality work, for fair and equitable taxation, for the protection of everyone's fundamental rights, for democracy, and for women's rights. Our collective action has highlighted the importance of strong social security and support for a just and sustainable transition that creates quality jobs by reducing inequalities. Our union has been fully committed to the fight for a reform of the Wage Norm Law, organizing demonstrations and presenting its arguments before Parliament. Our position is clear: we must return to an indicative wage norm that respects the right to free and fair collective bargaining.  The right to demonstrate or strike is fundamental, and any measure that may impact this right must be strictly limited and scrupulously comply with the principles of legality, proportionality, and necessity. In 2023, our collective freedoms were clearly threatened. Together with other unions, NGOs, and civil society organizations, we strongly opposed the bill that threatened the fundamental right to protest, emphasizing its danger to democracy. It took four mobilizations of tens of thousands of people for this bill to be definitively buried in January 2024. Our mobilization paid off!
