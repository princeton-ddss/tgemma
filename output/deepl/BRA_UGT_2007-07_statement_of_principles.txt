We, workers, gathered in São Paulo, capital, on July 19, 20, and 21, 2007, for the Founding Congress of the union - union - declare our principles, objectives, and commitments. Our common goal is to open a front in pursuit of profound changes in society, recognizing that there is a need for a new offensive historical cycle in the Brazilian labor movement.  In conceiving the idea of the union, we contribute to the construction of new paradigms, looking toward a new union project, consistent with the 21st century, centered on the human being and capable of offering answers and proposals to national problems.  We, in the union, in expressing our opposition to neoliberalism, unemployment, and low wages, condemn slave labor, child labor, and precarious work; we fight corruption and all forms of prejudice; we do not tolerate hunger and poverty; we will fight against the scrapping of education and health care; we are in favor of rebuilding Brazil's infrastructure and investing in research and development; we abhor the process of social, cultural, economic, and political exclusion, which is very present in the world of work and in the majority of our population; in short, we believe that our task of defending workers and the Brazilian people is much more than an ethical imperative; it is the basic condition for the construction and implementation of a democratic and transformative project for our country.
