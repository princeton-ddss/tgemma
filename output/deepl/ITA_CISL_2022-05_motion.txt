The Congress views with concern the current international scenario, which, now more than ever, calls into question the supreme good of world peace.  In this historical context, Europe is experiencing one of the most delicate moments since its establishment. In light of the situation created by the pandemic and the bloody invasion of Ukraine by the Russian regime, Europe must necessarily strengthen itself through a renewed commitment that focuses on solidarity among peoples and guarantees of freedom, hoping for a revision of the decision-making process from a unanimous system to a majority one.  In particular, it is necessary to continue to support expansionary economic policies in support of states, which should not be confined to the emergency phase alone, but directed towards the search for structural solutions that overcome the rigidity of the ‘stability pact’, consolidating Next Generation EU, resorting as soon as possible to the financing of a new ‘Sure Plan’ to support employment and preparing the strategies under RePower EU, which should be the first step towards achieving a unified energy strategy.  The renewed Union must strengthen its role by distinguishing itself through high social standards and consolidating the role of work and cohesion in the Community model, promoting the construction of a Community tax system and new economic partnership agreements between States.
