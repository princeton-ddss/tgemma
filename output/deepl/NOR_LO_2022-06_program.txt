Introduction  The union's goal is to increase union membership in Norway and to become the largest employee organization in all sectors and occupational groups. The strength and influence of the union lies in our large membership and broad organization, as well as our vibrant member democracy with active members and elected representatives who are given opportunities to influence and develop union policy. The union's presence through local organizations and regional forums is very important for achieving success in union policy.  This means that the unions must organize more members, and more unions must join the union family. This must be given particular priority in industries with low unionization rates. In order to maintain and strengthen the position of trade unions in Norwegian working life and society, union organization must adapt to changes in working life. Trade unions and federations must work to retain members in order to highlight their relevance to different types of workers.  The proportion of working people with higher education is increasing, and trade unions face strong competition from other confederations in this area. The goal is to strengthen the position and influence of trade unions within this group. Trade unions aim to be the preferred main organization for those with higher education as well.
