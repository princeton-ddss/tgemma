  Handling professional, settlement, and economic matters, as well as cultural and educational matters of the working class; ensuring organized labor in all workplaces, establishing professional associations for all workers in their respective professions, and establishing national unions in various industries. Encouraging immigration and its social and cultural integration. Promoting the national economy and increasing labor productivity and production.  Developing the workers' economy in agriculture (workers' settlements, groups, and kibbutzim), in industry and crafts, in construction, in transportation, and in other sectors of labor in the countryside and in the city.  Encouraging and developing cooperation in all its forms in all sectors of the economy.  Organizing workers' supply and consumption and marketing their products on a cooperative basis. Establishing institutions and funds to finance the workers' economy in agriculture, industry, and other sectors. Protecting consumers through consumer protection measures.  Establishing institutions and founding mutual aid funds: health funds, insurance and pension funds, and other funds designed to foster mutual aid among the working public.  Cultural and social activities among the working public: instilling the values of the labor movement, teaching the Hebrew language, publishing books and newspapers, Establishment of cultural and educational institutions and, in general, meeting the needs of religious union members, institutions for general education and professional training, encouraging physical culture among workers within the framework of the physical culture association “Yahapolei.”  Organization of the women workers' movement (union) as an integral part of the movement.
