1. Committed community on organization The level of unionization in the Danish labor market has been declining for over 25 years. Today, only 50% of the workforce is a member of a union under union or X, and in the private labor market, it is now more normal to be unorganized than organized.  It is up to the member organizations in the trade union to organize and retain more members, and this is a task that takes place every single day. However, there is a common challenge—across professions, education levels, industries, and age groups—that calls for a joint cross-cutting effort which, where relevant, can supplement the work of member organizations in reversing the trend in membership development.  Therefore, it is a common goal to reverse the membership trend for trade unions in the coming congress period.  In close cooperation with the member organizations, the union will therefore work to ensure that the community develops and launches a number of initiatives during the congress period that will contribute to the work of organizing and retaining more members in the union's member organizations.  A forum focusing on organizing and retention will be created, where knowledge and experience can be exchanged and developments followed up on.  The aim is also to follow up on existing analyses, prepare new ones, and launch new initiatives, among other things with a view to ensuring that fewer members fall between two stools, with a special focus on efforts targeting young people.
