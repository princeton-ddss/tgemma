The deficit in union activities remains. Previous fee committees had assumed that operating costs would be covered by membership fees, but found that this was not possible to combine with the ambitions of union activities. Over the past fifteen years, the finance committee has noted that trade unions must also finance parts of their activities with returns on capital, which continues to be a significant element in the financing of trade union activities.  In order to ensure the long-term viability of the union, the Fees Committee recommends a return to the fee model applied since 2009, with consistency between union resources and union membership fees, but that the cap applied following a decision at the 2016 congress be removed. The union's finance committee has annually reported proposals to the union's representative body regarding union fees. This has proven to be a robust system that has partially solved the problem of negative real development. The finance committee considers the current approach of alignment to be reasonable and positive and that it should continue during the congress period.
