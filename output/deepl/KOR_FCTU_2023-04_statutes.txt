Other necessary undertakings to achieve the union's objectives     Chapter 2 Organization     Article 5 (Composition)  ① The labor union shall be composed of industrial unions that endorse the labor union's declaration, program, and bylaws, and whose affiliation has been approved in accordance with these bylaws.  ② Industrial unions refer to nationwide industrial-level unit labor unions and federations; nationwide industrial councils of equivalent status, occupational labor unions, and general labor unions shall be considered affiliated units.  Article 6 (Labor Unions)  ① To activate operations in accordance with the Declaration, Program, Bylaws, and all policies in each region, labor unions shall establish labor unions as subordinate organizations based on metropolitan city/province standards and may establish labor unions as needed.  ② Labor unions shall be composed of unit labor unions or labor unions (labor unions, labor unions) in the relevant region.  ③ The organization and operation of labor unions shall be governed by separately established regulations.  Article 7 (Labor Unions)  ① Labor unions may establish labor unions as necessary to promote exchange, cooperation, and joint action among labor unions within the same or related industries.  ② The organization and operation of labor unions shall be governed by separately established regulations.  Article 8 (Affiliation and Withdrawal)  ① An industry-specific labor union wishing to affiliate with the labor union shall submit a prescribed application to the labor union and obtain its resolution.
