1.  A binding commitment to organization: The level of unionization in the Danish labor market has been declining for over 25 years. Today, only 50% of the workforce is a member of a trade union or affiliated organization, and in the private sector, it is now more common to be unorganized than organized. The member organizations of the trade union are responsible for recruiting and retaining more members, and this is a task that takes place every day. However, there is a common challenge – across professions, educational backgrounds, industries, and age groups – that calls for a joint, cross-cutting effort that, where relevant, can supplement the work of member organizations to reverse the trend of declining membership. Therefore, a common goal is to reverse the decline in membership for the trade union during the upcoming congress period. In close collaboration with the member organizations, the trade union will work to develop and implement a series of initiatives that will contribute to the efforts of member organizations to recruit and retain more members. A forum will be created with a focus on organization and retention, where knowledge and experiences are shared, and progress is monitored. The purpose is also to follow up on existing analyses and develop new ones, and to initiate new initiatives, including with the aim of ensuring that fewer members are lost and with a particular focus on initiatives targeted at young people.
