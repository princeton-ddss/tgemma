Providing professional support in matters related to employment, settlement, and economics, as well as addressing cultural and educational issues for the working population; ensuring organized labor in all workplaces, establishing professional organizations for all workers in their respective fields, and creating national trade unions in various industries. Encouraging immigration and promoting social and cultural integration. Strengthening the national economy and increasing productivity. Developing the workers' sector in agriculture (through workers' settlements, groups, and kibbutzim), in industry and crafts, in construction, transportation, and other industries in both rural and urban areas. Promoting and developing cooperatives in all its forms, across all sectors of the economy. Organizing the supply and consumption of goods for workers and marketing their products based on cooperative principles. Establishing institutions and creating funds to finance the workers' sector in agriculture, industry, and other fields. Protecting consumers through consumer protection measures. Establishing mutual aid institutions and funds: healthcare funds, insurance and pension funds, and other funds, designed to promote mutual support among the working population. Promoting cultural and social activities within the working community: instilling the values of the labor movement, promoting the Hebrew language, publishing literature and newspapers, establishing cultural and educational institutions, and meeting the needs of members of religious organizations. Providing general education and professional training. Encouraging physical activity among workers through the "Maccabi" sports organization. Organizing the women's movement (union) as an integral part of the organization. Supporting the pioneering spirit among youth and providing assistance to pioneering youth movements in the country, which educate their members in work, settlement, and membership in the labor federation. Maintaining connections with the diaspora.
