The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the importance of social cohesion, comprehensive social services, and a capable state. We have all experienced firsthand the value of solidarity and that this crisis can only be overcome together. As a trade union, we reacted quickly – we prioritized the health of workers, shaped the new working environment, and significantly influenced workplace safety and health measures. We achieved an increase and extension of short-time work benefits, and we initiated a broad debate in our country about the systemic importance and value of work. We ensured that parents who stayed at home during periods of homeschooling to care for their children did not suffer significant financial or professional disadvantages – and we demonstrated during the crisis that gender equality is a central element of our trade union work. We have repeatedly made it clear to politicians that our model of co-determination, collective bargaining agreements, and a strong trade union is a reliable anchor for the people in our country, especially during times of crisis.
