6. Expanding employee participation in management based on collective decision-making, and eliminating undemocratic elements in the workplace.
7. Improving working conditions, including securing a living wage, guaranteeing job security, reducing working hours, and expanding maternity protection.
8. Eliminating all forms of discrimination and achieving a safe and pleasant working environment.
9. Strengthening regulations on monopolies and protecting small and medium-sized enterprises and agriculture.
10. Reforming institutional and policy frameworks related to social security, housing, education, healthcare, taxation, finance, prices, banking, land, environment, and transportation.
11. Promoting international solidarity, human rights, and world peace.
12. Educational, propaganda, and publishing activities to achieve the aforementioned goals and objectives.
13. Other activities necessary to achieve the objectives of the labor union.

Chapter 2: Organization

Article 5 (Composition)
(1) The labor union shall be composed of industry-based labor unions that support the declaration, charter, and articles of the labor union and whose membership is approved in accordance with the provisions of these articles.
(2) An industry-based labor union refers to a national-level industry-based unit labor union and its affiliated organizations. Organizations with similar national-level industry-based associations, occupational labor unions, and general labor unions are considered affiliated units.

Article 6 (Regional Branches)
(1) To promote activities in each region in accordance with the declaration, charter, articles, and general policies, the labor union shall establish regional branches under its organization, based on the metropolitan and provincial level, and may establish additional regional branches as needed.
(2) A regional branch shall be composed of unit labor unions or affiliated organizations (labor unions, labor unions) in the respective region.
(3) The organization and operation of regional branches shall be governed by separately stipulated regulations.

Article 7 (Federations)
(1) To promote exchange, cooperation, and joint action among industry-based labor unions in the same or related industries, the labor union may establish federations as needed.
(2) The organization and operation of federations shall be governed by separately stipulated regulations.

Article 8 (Membership and Withdrawal)
(1) An industry-based labor union wishing to join the labor union shall submit the prescribed application to the labor union and obtain the approval of the labor union.
