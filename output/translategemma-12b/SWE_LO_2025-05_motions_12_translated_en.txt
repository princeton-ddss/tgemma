The deficit in the union's activities persists. Previous committees on membership fees had the initial premise that operating costs should be covered by membership fees, but they concluded that this was not possible to reconcile with the ambitions of the union's activities. For the past fifteen years, the finance committee has noted that the union must also finance part of its activities with investment income, which continues to be a significant component of funding for the union's operations. To ensure the long-term sustainability of the union, the committee on membership fees recommends a return to the fee model that has been in place since 2009, which aims for a balance between the union's resources and membership fees, but that the ceiling, which has been applied since the 2016 congress, be removed. The union's finance committee has annually presented proposals to the union's representatives regarding the union's fees. This has proven to be a robust system that has partially addressed the problem of the negative real-world impact. The finance committee believes that the current system, which aims for this balance, is reasonable and positive, and that it should continue during the current congress period.
