The trade union organization aims to actively participate in ensuring that labor law protects employees by providing them with a sufficient level of protection guaranteed by law, and that they have decent working conditions, including fair wages and salaries, safe working environments, equal treatment, opportunities for professional development, and the ability to balance their work and family life. The trade union organization is the largest organized interest group in Slovakia and is part of the largest labor movement in Europe and the world.

The trade union organization's priorities, based on its values and program principles, and its integration into European and national trade union structures, as well as various position papers and viewpoints, include so-called "traditional" (essential) issues such as wage increases, bonuses, the minimum wage, labor law legislation, the social security system (including the pension system), reducing working hours, balancing work and family life, a healthy work environment, improving the quality of the work environment and eliminating psychosocial risks in the workplace, social dialogue and collective bargaining, trade union rights, promoting employment, a labor-friendly tax policy, education policy, and its connection to the needs of the labor market, among others.
