Introduction: The union's goal is to increase its membership in Norway and to become the largest workers' organization across all sectors and professions. The union's strength and influence lie in its large membership base and its broad organization, along with a vibrant participatory democracy where active members and representatives are given the opportunity to influence and develop the union's policies. The union's presence through local organizations and regional forums is crucial for achieving its policy objectives. This means that the member organizations must recruit more members, and more organizations must join the union. This should be a particular priority in industries with low membership rates. To maintain and strengthen the union's position in Norwegian working and social life, its organization must adapt to changes in the workplace. The union and its member organizations must focus on member retention to demonstrate their relevance to various types of workers. The proportion of working professionals with higher education is increasing, and the union faces strong competition from other major organizations in this area. The goal is to strengthen the union's position and influence within this group. The union aims to be the preferred major organization, even for those with higher education.
