We, the workers, gathered in São Paulo, the capital, on July 19, 20, and 21, 2007, for the founding congress of the union, hereby declare our principles, objectives, and commitments. Our common goal is to open a new front to achieve profound changes in society, recognizing the need for a new, proactive phase in the Brazilian labor movement. We, by conceiving the idea of this union, are contributing to the construction of new paradigms, looking towards a new labor project that is in line with the 21st century, centered on the human being, and capable of offering solutions and proposals to national problems. We, as members of this union, by expressing our opposition to neoliberalism, unemployment, and low wages, condemn slavery, child labor, and precarious work; we fight against corruption and all forms of prejudice; we do not tolerate hunger and poverty; we will fight against the deterioration of education and healthcare; we advocate for the restoration of Brazil's infrastructure and investments in research and development; we abhor the process of social, cultural, economic, and political exclusion, which is prevalent in the world of work and among the majority of our population. Ultimately, we believe that our task of defending workers and the Brazilian people is more than just an ethical imperative; it is the fundamental condition for building and implementing a democratic and transformative project for our country.
