We have fought for recognized and quality work, for a fair and equitable tax system, for the protection of everyone's fundamental rights, for democracy, and for women's rights. Our collective action has highlighted the importance of a strong social safety net and a just and sustainable transition that creates quality jobs while reducing inequalities. Marie-Hélène Ska, Secretary General, has dedicated herself wholeheartedly to the fight for a reform of the law on wage standards, organizing demonstrations and presenting our arguments to Parliament. Our position is clear: we must return to an indicative wage standard that respects the right to free and fair collective bargaining. The right to demonstrate or strike is fundamental, and any measure that could impact this right must be strictly limited and scrupulously adhere to the principles of legality, proportionality, and necessity. In 2023, our collective freedoms were clearly threatened. Together with other unions, NGOs, and civil society organizations, we strongly opposed the bill that threatened the fundamental right to demonstrate, emphasizing its danger to democracy. It took four mobilizations involving tens of thousands of people for this bill to be definitively buried in January 2024. Our mobilization paid off!
