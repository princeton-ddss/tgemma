The Congress is deeply concerned about the current international landscape, which, more than ever before, challenges the ultimate goal of peace in the world. In this historical context, Europe is experiencing one of the most delicate periods since its formation. Given the circumstances created by the pandemic and the bloody invasion of Ukraine by the Russian regime, Europe must necessarily strengthen itself through a renewed commitment that prioritizes solidarity among peoples and guarantees of freedom. We hope for a revision of the decision-making process, moving from a system of unanimity to a majority-based system.

In particular, it is necessary to continue supporting expansionary economic policies to support countries, policies that should not be limited to the emergency phase, but should be directed towards finding structural solutions that overcome the rigidity of the "stability pact." It is crucial to consolidate the Next Generation EU initiative, as soon as possible, by funding a new "Sure" plan to support employment and by developing the strategies outlined in the RePower EU program, which should be the first step towards creating a unified energy strategy.

The renewed European Union must strengthen its role by establishing high social standards and consolidating the role of work and cohesion within the European model. This should involve promoting the creation of a European tax system and new economic partnership agreements between countries.
