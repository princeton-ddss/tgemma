Program Statement. In response to the social, economic, and advocacy challenges of the 21st century, which are different from what was previously customary, and in order to increase the efficiency of trade union work to protect the interests of employees, the trade union is establishing a trade union federation. With this federation, the three confederations are creating a trade union federation that brings together the majority of organized workers in the current Hungarian trade union movement. The new organization, and with it the complete unification of the three current founding organizations, represents not only responsibility but also a serious commitment. Our primary commitment is to represent the interests of trade union members, the entire workforce, including those in the public and private sectors, civil servants, public employees, members of the armed forces and law enforcement agencies, those who have lost their jobs, people with disabilities, students, and retirees, as well as to strengthen civil society and democratic Hungary. Our union is based on fundamental values such as solidarity, equal opportunity, democracy, patriotism, Europeanism, the rule of law, and political neutrality. The three confederations and their member organizations that are founding the federation believe in and are committed to upholding these values. We believe that only on the basis of these values can a modern Hungary of the 21st century be built. Our federation's policies will take into account the common values and goals of the democratic countries that make up the European Union, while respecting our national historical traditions. We want a Hungary that, within a civil democratic framework, respects the freedom of private property and enterprise, and is able to establish and operate a social and economic dialogue, and where necessary, legally guarantee it, so that workers and the broadest segments of society can benefit from the results achieved, regardless of their current situation. Therefore, the trade union wants to create a qualitatively different relationship with the branches of government, the government, employers, and civil society in Hungary. Our size and our social embeddedness entitle us to do this. We believe that we are not alone in the world, and we are calling on our partners to cooperate, and through our member organizations, we are ready to continuously expand our network and reach out to new social groups. The trade union federation we have created is based on the principle of voluntarism. This must apply to the world of work and in the work of our member organizations. To this end, the trade union will establish internal cooperation that respects the freedom of organization and the autonomy of member organizations and sectors, and that will ensure effective advocacy. We believe that the interests of our members are clearly reflected in the sectoral organizations of the advocacy system. The task of the trade union is to support the work of these sectoral organizations. The trade union represents the interests of workers in the public and private sectors without discrimination. We want to work more effectively, harmonizing and strengthening the will and intentions of these two large groups, and represent their interests. In this way, we will be able to achieve coordinated representation of common interests in the existing forums for dialogue and to achieve effective and broad internal cooperation. At the same time, we believe it is important to emphasize that we consider it essential to complete the macro-level social dialogue. We want to agree on its implementation with all employers' and employee advocacy organizations and the government, taking into account the jointly established criteria for representativeness. The trade union seeks to establish a strong and influential presence in the domestic advocacy system. To this end, it provides effective expert support and a visible local, regional, and international presence to its member organizations. We believe that the trade union is capable of opening a new chapter in the Hungarian trade union movement in the 21st century. The trade union undertakes to preserve the intellectual heritage of the more than one and a half centuries of the Hungarian trade union movement, including the valuable experiences and previous advocacy work of the three founding organizations. The new trade union federation, with the adopted documents, including this program statement, is not concluding but launching the implementation of a modern and unified employee advocacy organization that we believe is essential for building a modern Hungary.
